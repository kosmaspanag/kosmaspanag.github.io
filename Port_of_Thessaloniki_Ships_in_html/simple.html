Port of Thessaloniki

Fluctuation of ship routes towards one of the one of the largest Greek seaports and one of the largest ports in the Aegean Sea basin.

By Kosmas Panagiotidis

Published July 17, 2019

After the Port of Piraeus is the second largest container port in Greece. In Fig 1(Greek_ships_year) we can see the total number of Greek ships that came into the port per year.

Even more clearly, we can see the fluctuation in the Fig.2 (Greek_ships_year2) where from 2009 to 2013 the number of the ships was decreasing every year. But from 2014 till 2018 there was a major increase.

The Greek fleet is one of the biggest in Europe. This is one of many reasons that most of the ships which came in and out of the port were Greek. However Fig.3 shows the total number of ships per year from all the countries.

Statistical data from 2009 to 2018 show that 19.281 ships from 34 countries went into the Port of Thessaloniki. The graphical data show exactly why this port is a major maritime hub.
